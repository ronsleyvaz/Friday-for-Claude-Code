# Why the Week Slips Away (and How a Weekly Review Stops It)

The week ends and you cannot say with confidence what you shipped. You have a rough sense of what happened. You know you were busy. But the wins are blurry, the misses are soft-pedalled in your head, and whatever was supposed to carry forward is sitting in a mental pile somewhere.

The Weekly Review command turns a blur into a record.

---

## What the review captures

Four things. In order.

**Wins.** Named specifically. Not "had some good calls." Which calls. What landed. What shipped. What moved. The specificity matters because vague wins do not compound. A win you can name in one line is a win you can build on.

**Misses.** Without softening. A miss is a miss. The review is not the place for context and explanation. Note it. Move on. The pattern across weeks is where the useful information lives.

**Carry-forwards.** Everything that moves into next week. If the same item has carried forward for three weeks, the review surfaces that. You either decide it or you stop pretending it is on the list.

**One priority.** Not three. Not five. One. The most consequential thing for the week ahead. Not the loudest. Not the most urgent. The one that, done well, makes the most difference.

---

## Why one priority

Founders end the week with a list and start the next one already behind. A list of priorities is not a priority.

Naming one forces a real choice. You can have a list and also one priority. But if you name ten priorities, you have named zero.

---

## Why the record matters

`friday/review.md` grows with every run. After four weeks, you have a pattern. After twelve, you have a real picture of where your time and attention go.

That record is the most honest performance data a founder can have. It is not aggregated or filtered. It is what you said happened, week by week, unfiltered.

---

## What the review does not do

This command does not connect to your task manager. It does not pull from your calendar or inbox. You bring the raw material.

The full stack is Friday at friday.amplifyais.com. Your completed tasks, meetings, and decisions pulled automatically. The review is ready when you sit down.

---

## The growing config

The Weekly Review adds `friday/review.md` to the folder you have been building.

Your voice profile. Your decision log. Your nine recurring decisions. Your morning brief. Your meeting log. Now your weekly review. Each file is a real output on your machine, not a product promise.
