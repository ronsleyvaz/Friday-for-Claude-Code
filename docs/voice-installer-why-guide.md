## What voice actually is

Your voice is not tone. It is not personality. It is the specific way you structure a sentence, the phrases you reach for without thinking, and the words you would never write.

Three things make your voice yours:

**Rhythm.** How long your sentences run. Shorter is almost always clearer. If your samples average 12 words a sentence and ChatGPT ships 28, readers feel the difference without knowing why.

**Signature phrases.** The words and constructions you repeat. "Here is the thing." "Right now." "Straight up." These are not filler. They are anchors. Readers who follow you long enough learn to recognise them.

**What you refuse.** Every voice has a banned list. Words that sound like someone else. Consulting-deck words. 2003-boardroom words. Wellness-brand words. When these appear in your writing, your readers feel it, even if they cannot name it.

## The six writing modes (know which one you are in)

Founders write in two modes, marketing and email, and ignore the other four. That is where the inconsistency creeps in.

**Transactional:** short, functional, no personality required. Receipts, confirmations, status updates. No voice needed.

**Personal:** direct address. You to them, about something real. This is where your signature phrases earn their keep.

**Commercial / nurture:** drip emails, onboarding sequences, follow-ups after a call. Warm, specific, always front-loaded. The P.S. is not optional.

**Campaign / broadcast:** posts, threads, newsletters. Hook in the first two lines. Every paragraph earns the next. No sign-off platitudes.

**Internal / team:** chat, standups, shared comments. Drop the register, keep the precision.

**Doc mode:** SOP, guide, explainer. Write like you are talking to someone on their first day. Active voice. Short steps. No walls of prose.

The Voice Installer captures your voice at the profile level. Each mode then applies that profile to its own register. Same person, six contexts.

## The banned words list (and why it matters more than your bio)

Every AI voice profile starts with a banned list. Not a style guide. Not a tone document. A list of words the founder refuses.

These fall into three categories. Consulting-deck words that got absorbed into corporate writing in the 1990s. AI-training words that appear at high frequency in blog posts and make every model reach for them first. And wellness-brand words that sound positive but carry no information.

They are not wrong words. They are borrowed words. They came from someone else's writing, trained into every AI tool, and now they come out in yours too.

The Voice Installer ships with 20 of these pre-loaded. It also asks you to add your own. The words your team uses that make you cringe. The phrases you keep cutting from drafts. Those go on your list.

The fix is mechanical. You cannot feel yourself reaching for a consulting-deck word in the moment. You have to scan for it after. Or you install a tool that does it for you.
